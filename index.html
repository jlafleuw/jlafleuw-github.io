Jody's HCDE 533 Digital Fabrication Page

My name is Jody Laflen. I'm in my second year as an HCDE masters student and excited to learn all about digital fabrication. I know this will be challenging, since I do not already know how to work in CAD, but I will give it my best.

Assignment 1: Cardboard Catastrophe
Assignment 1 was supposed to be a pumpkin.

Assignment 2: Jumpy with Grasshopper
Assignment 2 used grasshopper to make 3D pieces to interlock into an abstract sculpture.

Assignment 3: Getting Started with 3D Printing
(Or How I Learned to Stop Printing in 2D and Started 3D Printing All The Things)
Assignment 3 was an exercise in assembling the Ender-3 Pro printer, learning how to level test, and then, using Rhino and Cura, figure out how to print a series of shapes with specific parameters.

Assignment 4: Utter Chaos (a.k.a. Subtractive Manufacturing)
While everything in this assignment that could go wrong did, I managed to struggle through with a sort of box. I went to the Mill twice to try my box pattern on the CNC mill, but could not get things to work out, so I resorted to the 3D printer due to lack of time.

Assignment 5: Meshing Around
This was a fun assignment, downloading and playing with different mesh objects and mesh types before deciding on what pairing I wanted to make and print. I ran into some problems when I had to figure out how to fix the extruder and replace the nozzle, but I did that, too!

Illuminating the World Around Me (Making a Lamp)
This is the first half of my work toward building a lamp, I have been experimenting with styles, and figuring out, with my current skill set, what I can realistically make (while still challenging myself).
